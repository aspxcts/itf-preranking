How to use this tool

What is this?

This tool shows current ITF Junior rankings and projects live ranking positions in real time during a tournament week — before the official ITF list is updated. Hover over any column heading for a quick explanation.

Rankings tab

Shows the current official ITF rankings for the week.

  • Rank — Official ITF ranking position at the start of this week.
  • Movement — ITF's published rank movement from the previous week (▲ = climbed, ▼ = dropped).
  • Tournament — Tournament(s) the player competed in this week.
  • Born — Player's birth year.
  • Points — Official ITF ranking points total.

Click any row to open the player's full breakdown and What-if simulator.

Live Rankings tab

Shows live projected rankings based on this week's in-progress tournament results. Players with red badges next to their name are losing points as an old result expires off their 52-week total. If historical non-countable results slide up to replace the expiring ones, the replacement amount is shown in green next to the expiry value.

  • Rank — Official ITF rank at the start of the week.
  • Live Rank — Projected position based on results so far this week.
  • ±Rank — Movement between official rank and live rank. Green ▲ = climbed, Red ▼ = dropped.
  • Pts Gained — Net points gained this week (new results minus any expiring results).
  • Live Total — Projected ranking points total after this week.

Tournaments tab

Shows all active tournaments this week. Click a card to open the draw bracket. Use the search bar to quickly find a specific player across all tournaments.

How points work

  • Each player's best 6 singles results count toward their singles ranking total.
  • Doubles points contribute at 25% of their value toward the combined total.
  • Results older than 52 weeks automatically expire and are removed.
  • The combined ranking = Singles total + Doubles total × 0.25.

What-if simulator

Inside any tournament's bracket modal, switch to the What-if tab to simulate hypothetical results for any player and see how their ranking would change.

Data freshness

The Updated badge at the top of the page shows when data was last fetched from the ITF website. Data refreshes automatically every 12 hours via a scheduled pipeline. If it turns amber, the data is older than 24 hours — this can happen if the server is restarting or ITF's site is temporarily unavailable. While the pipeline is running the badge will show the current phase.
